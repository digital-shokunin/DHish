# GM Procedures

## Session Prep Checklist
1. Review active campaign countdowns and advance as needed
2. Review PC connections, backgrounds, and recent choices for hooks
3. Prep 2-3 NPCs with motivations and distinct voices
4. Prepare 1-2 encounter options (build with Battle Points if combat)
5. Note current Hope/Fear totals from last session (both carry over)
6. Have 2-3 scene hooks ready; plan a strong opening moment

## Session Start Sequence
1. **Recap**: Summarize last session; ask players what they remember
2. **Establish scene**: Describe the current situation, location, and sensory details
3. **Offer choices**: Present 2-3 clear options or an open prompt ("What do you do?")
4. **Set the tone**: Match energy to the narrative beat (tense, relaxed, urgent)

## Combat Procedure

### Entering Combat
1. Describe the threat and establish relative positions (use range bands)
2. Players act first by default
3. Any player can volunteer to take the spotlight

### Spotlight Flow
1. **Player turn**: PC describes action, makes rolls as needed
   - Free movement within Close range as part of action
   - Further movement requires Agility Roll
   - On **Success with Hope**: Resolve, player gains Hope, spotlight stays with players
   - On **Success with Fear**: Resolve with complication, GM gains Fear, spotlight may swing
   - On **Failure**: Consequence occurs, spotlight swings to GM
   - On **Critical Success**: Auto-success + bonus, gain Hope + clear Stress
2. **GM turn** (on Fear/failure): Take adversary actions
   - Each active adversary: move (Close) + attack or use Action feature
   - Spend Fear for extra actions, Fear Features, or complications
   - Return spotlight to players after GM actions resolve
3. **Repeat** until combat ends

### Damage Resolution (for adversary attacks on PCs)
1. Determine total damage dealt
2. Check PC's armor thresholds: below Major = 1 HP, Major-to-Severe = 2 HP, at/above Severe = 3 HP
3. PC may mark an Armor Slot to reduce severity by one level
4. Mark the resulting HP
5. If last HP marked: PC makes a Death Move

### Ending Combat
- Combat ends when adversaries are defeated, flee, or surrender
- Or when the narrative situation resolves the conflict
- No formal end-of-combat phase; transition to exploration/roleplay naturally

## Social Encounter Guidance
- Set Difficulty for persuasion/deception based on NPC disposition and stakes
- Use the spotlight system even in social scenes -- let each PC contribute
- NPCs have motivations; appeals that align succeed more easily
- Social adversaries (1 BP) can be used for structured social challenges
- Track consequences: NPCs remember how they were treated

## Exploration & Travel Procedure
1. Describe the environment and available paths
2. Ask players for their approach and formation
3. Call for rolls when danger or uncertainty exists (Instinct for perception, Agility for navigation, Knowledge for lore)
4. Use countdowns for travel (tick per leg of journey)
5. Introduce 1-2 encounters or discoveries per travel segment
6. Short rests during travel are natural pacing points

## NPC Management
- Give each NPC a **motivation** (what they want) and a **method** (how they pursue it)
- Use distinct speaking patterns or mannerisms for memorable NPCs
- Scale information: NPCs share what they know based on trust and relevance
- Recurring NPCs should react to PC actions between sessions
- Keep a brief NPC log: name, role, disposition toward party, last interaction

## Pacing Guidelines
- Alternate between tension and release (combat -> roleplay -> exploration)
- Use countdowns to create urgency without railroading
- A typical session: 1 major encounter + 2-3 roleplay/exploration scenes
- If energy flags, introduce a twist or skip to the next interesting moment
- End sessions on a hook: cliffhanger, revelation, or difficult choice

## Hope/Fear Economy Management
- **Fear pool near 0**: Players are in control; let them enjoy it but prepare for the next challenge. Rests will replenish.
- **Fear pool at 6-8**: Healthy tension; spend on varied moves (not just combat).
- **Fear pool near 12 (max)**: Spend aggressively on dramatic moves; avoid hoarding.
- **Hope economy**: If players hoard Hope, create situations where spending feels rewarding. If Hope is scarce, ease difficulty slightly.
- Remember: every rest gives you Fear. Short rest = 1d4. Long rest = 1d4 + number of PCs. Use this to stay engaged.
- Spend Fear on narrative moves (categories 1-5) as much as combat moves (category 6) for richer sessions.
