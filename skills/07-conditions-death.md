# Conditions and Death

## Three Conditions

| Condition | Effect | Cleared By |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Hidden** | Location unknown; rolls against you have disadvantage | Enemies see you, you attack, or enter line of sight |
| **Restrained** | Cannot move but can still act from position | Successful action roll or narrative moment |
| **Vulnerable** | All rolls targeting you have advantage | Successful action roll or narrative moment |

Temporary conditions can be cleared by making successful action rolls or at narrative moments determined by the GM.

## Death Move

Triggered when a PC marks their **last Hit Point**. Player chooses one:

### 1. Blaze of Glory
- Take one final action (auto-crits with GM approval)
- Then **die permanently**
- No coming back -- this is a heroic sacrifice

### 2. Avoid Death
- Fall unconscious
- GM describes the situation worsening
- Cannot move, act, or be targeted
- Regain consciousness when an ally clears 1+ HP on you, or after a long rest
- **Scar check**: Roll Hope Die. If value <= character level, gain a **Scar**
- If last Hope slot is scarred, character **dies**

### 3. Risk It All
- Roll Duality Dice (no modifier, no difficulty)
- **Hope die higher**: Stay up, clear HP or Stress = Hope die value
- **Fear die higher**: **Die**
- **Matching**: Stay up, clear nothing

## Scar Mechanics

- Triggered on Avoid Death: roll Hope Die, if result <= character level, gain a Scar
- A Scar permanently crosses out one Hope slot (reducing max Hope)
- Narrative impact: the Scar should reflect the near-death experience
- If a character's last remaining Hope slot is crossed out by a Scar, the character **dies permanently**
- Scars cannot be healed or removed
- Higher-level characters are more likely to scar (level acts as threshold)
