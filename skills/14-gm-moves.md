# GM Moves

## When to Make Moves
Make a GM move when:
- A player rolls **with Fear** (Fear die > Hope die)
- A player **fails** an action roll (spotlight swings to you)
- An action has obvious consequences
- A **golden opportunity** presents itself
- Players look to you for what happens next

## Player-Facing Rolls
You (the GM) **do not roll dice**. All rolls are made by players. When adversaries attack, either:
- Apply damage directly during your spotlight turn
- Have the target PC make a reaction roll to avoid/reduce damage

## Fear-Spending Move Categories

### 1. Environmental Trouble (1+ Fear)
- Weather shifts dangerously
- Structural collapse or terrain hazards
- Flora/fauna disturbance
- Magical wards fail or trigger
- Ground gives way, fire spreads, water rises

### 2. Resource Pressure (1+ Fear)
- Supplies spoil or are lost
- Tools break at critical moments
- Unexpected costs or tolls
- Sickness or exhaustion sets in
- Equipment is stolen or damaged

### 3. Social Instability (1+ Fear)
- Party's reputation takes a hit
- Authorities grow suspicious
- Alliances strain under pressure
- NPCs waver in loyalty or commitment
- Rumors spread, trust erodes

### 4. Pressure on Party (1+ Fear)
- Consequences from past choices arrive
- Personal fears or flaws manifest
- Tempting shortcuts with hidden costs
- Moral dilemmas forced by circumstance
- Loved ones threatened or endangered

### 5. Escalations (1+ Fear)
- Hints of future doom intensify
- Factions act off-screen
- Campaign countdowns advance
- Opportunities are lost forever
- Reinforcements arrive for adversaries

### 6. Combat Enhancements (1 Fear each)
- Place 2 additional action tokens for adversary actions
- Activate adversary Fear Features
- Add adversary Experience bonus to attacks or raise Difficulty
- End a PC's maintained spell
- Call reinforcements or change battlefield conditions

## Other Fear Costs
- **Advance a countdown**: Varies by countdown
- **End maintained spell**: 1 Fear
- **Extra adversary actions**: 1 Fear for 2 tokens

## Move Principles
- Moves should feel like natural consequences, not arbitrary punishment
- Escalate gradually -- minor complications before major crises
- Telegraph dangers before they strike when possible
- Always leave a path forward, even when things go badly
