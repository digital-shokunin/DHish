# Campaign Structure

## 10 Levels Across 4 Tiers

| Tier | Levels | Adversary Tier | Difficulty |
|------|--------|---------------|-----------|
| 1 | 1 | 0 | 11 |
| 2 | 2-4 | 1 | 14 |
| 3 | 5-7 | 2 | 17 |
| 4 | 8-10 | 3 | 20 |

## Milestone Leveling
- Party levels at **GM-determined narrative milestones** (typically every ~3 sessions)
- All party members level simultaneously

## Level-Up Steps

### 1. Tier Achievements (at levels 2, 5, 8 only)
- Gain new Experience (+2)
- +1 Proficiency
- Clear marked traits (levels 5 and 8 only)

### 2. Choose 2 Advancements (from current tier or below)
- Increase two unmarked traits by +1 each
- Add 1+ HP slots
- Add 1+ Stress slots
- Increase two Experiences by +1 each
- Acquire domain card (at/below level; multiclass cards at half level)
- Increase Evasion by +1
- Take upgraded subclass card (Foundation -> Specialization -> Mastery)
- Increase Proficiency (costs **2** advancement slots)
- Multiclass (costs **2** slots; available level 5+)

### 3. Damage Thresholds
All thresholds increase by 1 per level (added to armor base values).

### 4. Domain Cards
Acquire one new card at or below current level from class domains.

## Countdown Mechanics

| Type | Behavior |
|------|----------|
| **Standard** | Starts at N, ticks down by 1 each time a player makes an action roll (regardless of result). Effect triggers at 0. |
| **Dynamic** | Ticks based on player roll outcomes or specific choices, not automatically. |
| **Campaign** | Tracks long-term threats (politics, faction schemes, wars). Ticks session-by-session or on long rests. |

Use countdowns to create urgency, track off-screen events, and pace encounters.
